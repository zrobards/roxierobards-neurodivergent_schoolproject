Connections

Neurodiversity and mental health can overlap, but they are not the same.

Neurodivergence is neurodevelopmental. Mental health experiences like anxiety or depression can affect anyone. Understanding both reduces stigma and supports wellbeing.

Key distinctions

  • Neurodiversity: how brains develop and process information.
  • Mental health: emotional and psychological wellbeing that can change over time.
  • People can be neurodivergent, have a mental health condition, both, or neither.

Anxiety

Worry and physical tension can show up for anyone. Clear expectations, breathing breaks, and supportive peers help.

Depression

Low mood or energy may make tasks feel heavy. Gentle check-ins, small steps, and rest can be supportive.

Burnout

Prolonged stress or sensory overload can drain energy. Reducing demands, adding recovery time, and simplifying tasks are helpful.

Stress

Everyone experiences stress. Predictability, kind communication, and flexible timelines make stress easier to manage.

How overlap can happen

  • Masking or hiding differences can be exhausting and lead to burnout.
  • Sensory overload can raise stress and anxiety.
  • Stigma or misunderstandings may impact mood and belonging.
  • Lack of accommodations can create avoidable pressure.

Supportive approaches

  • Ask, "What would help right now?" and offer choices.
  • Break tasks into steps with clear, written instructions.
  • Provide quiet spaces and sensory breaks without judgment.
  • Encourage rest, hydration, movement, and community support.

Respectful language

Use person-first or identity-first language based on what individuals prefer. Avoid labels like "broken" or "burden." Center dignity, autonomy, and consent.
