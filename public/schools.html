Supportive environments

Schools can welcome neurodiverse students with simple, thoughtful steps.

Flexibility, clear communication, and sensory-aware spaces help everyone learn.

Classroom practices

  • Provide clear written instructions and examples.
  • Offer flexible deadlines when possible.
  • Allow movement breaks and alternative seating.
  • Share agendas early so students can prepare.

Sensory-friendly spaces

  • Quiet corners or rooms for cooldowns.
  • Lighting options: lamps instead of bright fluorescents when possible.
  • Headphone-friendly policies for independent work.
  • Minimize loud bells or give advance warnings.

Inclusive communication

  • Use clear, direct language without sarcasm or ambiguity.
  • Offer choices: oral, written, or visual instructions.
  • Respect preferred names, pronouns, and communication styles.
  • Invite feedback: "What helps you learn best?"

Community ideas

  • Peer buddy systems for transitions or new routines.
  • Clubs and interest groups that value different talents.
  • Teacher training on neurodiversity and sensory differences.
  • Celebrating strengths: art, coding, music, design, strategy games.
