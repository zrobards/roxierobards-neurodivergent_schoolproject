Tools to try

Coping strategies you can mix, match, and adapt.

Not every strategy works for everyone â€” and that is okay. Choose what feels respectful and sustainable.

Emotional coping

  • Mindfulness: Notice five things you see, four you feel, three you hear, two you smell, one you taste.
  • Journaling: Write two sentences about what went well and one thing you want to try differently.
  • Grounding: Hold a cold object or splash water on your hands to reset.

Breathing reset

Inhale for 4 seconds, pause for 2, exhale for 6. Repeat three times.

Micro-breaks

Stretch for one minute or walk to refill water. Short breaks can prevent overload.

Executive function

  • Use planners or phone reminders for deadlines.
  • Break tasks into three tiny steps; start with the smallest.
  • Timers (like 10 minutes on, 2 minutes off) to manage pacing.
  • Lay out materials the night before to reduce decision fatigue.

Sensory regulation

  • Noise-canceling or soft headphones for busy spaces.
  • Sensory breaks in a calm corner or quiet hallway.
  • Adjust lighting: softer lamps instead of bright overheads if possible.
  • Carry a texture item (smooth stone, fabric) for grounding.

Three-step task start

Sensory calm space

Create a quick comfort zone when sound, light, or texture feels intense.

Respectful pause script

Use a short script to step away, breathe, and return to the conversation.

Social tools

  • Set boundaries: "I need a five-minute break" or "Can we continue later?"
  • Identify safe people you can text or sit with during busy times.
  • Ask for help with a script: "I am stuck on step two; can you show me once?"
  • Practice transitions: preview what is next and what is expected.
